About Me

My history with computers and coding is very limited. For most of my childhood, we owned a home computer -
but it was used for my mom's work, so we weren't allowed to use it.

One thing I did always have an interest in though, was videogames.

Ever since I was 4 or 5, I always dreamed of creating my own one day.

But being 5 years old, I had no idea coding even existed, so I focused more on the art side of it.

Fast forward to college in 2006, and while still loving video games, my interest in art had waned, due to bad experiences with teachers in school over the years.

So I moved on to studying things like psychology, biology, and history.

This period of my life was my first introduction to coding.

Due to the myspace era of the internet, dabbling with basic html to add videos and backgrounds to my profile was a fun activity, but I never took it anywhere outside of myspace.

After college I revisited my art, and decided to start drawing comics.

Which ended up being my second experience with coding.

Styling websites to host my comics on was, again, a fun activity, but once again I never took it anywhere.

During this time I also managed to start an ebay business focused around painting and selling miniatures (Warhammer, dungeons and dragons, etc)

It wasn't exactly a high paying endevour, but it still managed to sustain me for many years.

Around 2014 I ended up putting comics on hold to focus more on ebay.

In around 2017 I started making videos on youtube, and later, livestreaming on twitch.

This was my third experience with coding.

Styling profiles, and editing code for streaming widgets.

And once again, i never took it anywhere.

Until the pandemic hit that is, and my ebay business was no longer able to support me.

Various pieces of worldwide politics since 2016, mixed with the pandemic, all but officially spelt the end for my 10 year business.

It was time for something new, to better myself for a changing landscape, and coding popped back into my head.

Having always been interested in computers and video games, and how they all worked, I decided to finally learn coding.

I was quickly introduced to the netmatters scion scheme, and I jumped at the chance to learn, which is where I'm at today.
